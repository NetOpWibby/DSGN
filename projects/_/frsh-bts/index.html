Project · Website

In the beginning, "Fresh Beats" was a collaboration between myself and a buddy in high school (circa 2003). We wanted to be the Northeast version of The Neptunes. Pharrell and Chad inspired us so much and we loved their beats.

After graduation my buddy disappeared and I experimented with beat-making, music production, and graphic design. As I experimented with web design, "Web 2.0" hit and I removed vowels from the name to make it look cooler (that was the trend and honestly, it does look cooler). I think the second website I ever built was for FRSH×BTS.

For the past few years, I would redesign the site and use it for a couple months before abandoning it, and the cycle would continue. I blamed my bad design skills, the clunkiness of Wordpress, &c. What I have come to realize with the current (2014) iteration is that every site I built simply did not fit the concept of FRSH×BTS.

I rap spit hot fiyah under the aliases of "the Wibby" and "Spaceman Fresh" (y'know, like Jay Z is also "Hova"). And yet, the main focus of every past site was the blog, where I would post interesting interviews of my favorite artists and music videos. You must be thinking to yourself, "Well that's dumb". I wholeheartedly agree, so I did something about it.

The Process

Some people like using project management software and mockup apps for projects, but you can't replace good ol' pen and paper when "in the moment" inspiration strikes.

When you arrive at the website, you are on #first-level and you see #frsh, the logo. When you click on the logo, #first-level is zoomed in and disappears. At the same time, #single-level zooms in from the center of the screen to full size. I wanted to give visitors the feeling that they are discovering what FRSH×BTS is about, on the microscopic level.
